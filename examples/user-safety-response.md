# Example: User Safety Response

An illustrative reply to a worried end user who may have used a scam site. Plain
language, calm, and safe. Fictional.

## At a glance

```text
DO NOW
  1. Disconnect your wallet and stop signing.
  2. Use only the official site — type the address yourself.
  3. Save the signature, the URL you used, and the time.

DON'T
  • Never share your seed phrase or private key — no one legit will ask.
  • Don't trust a DM offering to "recover" your funds.

SAFE TO SHARE
  • transaction signature · the URL · wallet app · approx time
```

## Reply

```text
Thanks for reaching out, and take a breath — let's go step by step.

Right now:
1. Disconnect your wallet from that site and stop signing anything.
2. Use only our official site by typing the address yourself; don't click links
   from DMs.
3. Save your evidence: the transaction signature, the exact URL you used, your
   wallet app, and the time.

Please do not:
- Share your seed phrase or private key with anyone — ever. No real team or tool
  will ask for them.
- Trust anyone who DMs you offering to "recover" your funds. That's a scam
  pattern; real support won't DM you first or send recovery links.

If you entered your seed phrase on that site, treat that wallet as compromised:
move your assets to a brand-new wallet using your normal, trusted process.

If you can share the transaction signature and the URL you used, we can help
check what happened. You won't need to share anything secret to do that.
```

## Why this is safe

- Never asks for secrets; warns about seed phrases and fake-support DMs.
- Gives clear, ordered, low-risk actions.
- Makes no promise that funds are recoverable. See
  [`../skill/modes/user-safety-mode.md`](../skill/modes/user-safety-mode.md).
